Ready to get wet ‘n’ wild? Here are the best water activities in Hong Kong

by Elaine Wong • 15 Sep 2021

When you’re bored with the beach and the pool, cool off with these other water activities in Hong Kong!

Been there, done that with all the nice pools and beaches in Hong Kong? Don’t fret, there are many other places for you to make a splash, without having to tackle water sports or break a sweat. Plus, most of these venues are kid-friendly, making them ideal destinations for you and your family. So, grab your goggles – let’s dive into the best water activities in Hong Kong.

Make a splash with these water activities in Hong Kong

1. Mermaid Federation International (MFI)

Ever wondered what it’s like to be a mermaid? You can now live your mermaid dreams with MFI, a mermaid ‘school’ based in Hong Kong. Aiming to raise public awareness on ocean conservation and water safety practices, MFI offers a Mermaid Development Programme, which is a structured curriculum led by professional mermaid instructors. Whether you’re an absolute beginner, or working your way up to become a Master Mermaid, MFI will guide you through having the best (and prettiest!) time in the water.

Find out more about Mermaid Federation International (MFI) (國際人魚聯盟).

2. Dream Cruises

International travel may not be possible right now, but the good news is that you can still set foot outside Hong Kong with a seacation on Genting HK’s Dream Cruises! The Genting Dream Super Seacation (setting sail until 31 October 2021) is one of its latest offerings, during which you can enjoy all sorts of entertainment, including an indoor arcade, cinema, a glowing bowling alley, and even a giant outdoor water slide. There are also themed events onboard, including a Japanese Festival, the Oktoberfest, and other seasonal activities all year round.

Find out more about Dream Cruises (星夢郵輪).

3. Hong Kong Disneyland

Disneyland is more than just Mickey and Minnie Mouse! Head over to the Adventureland, the tropical forest zone on the southern side of the Park. Go on a thrilling expedition aboard the Jungle River Cruise, though do be warned that you may get splashed by playful animals along the way. If you’re looking for something a little less wild, visit the Liki Tikis totem poles. In this mini oasis, you’ll be able to cool down as the poles spray mist and water on unsuspecting passers-by. In addition, you can go paddle-boating at Inspiration Lake Recreation Centre, which lies around one kilometre outside of the Park. Take in the scenery of the surrounding mountains, the shimmering manmade lake, and the dynamic fountain.

Hong Kong Disneyland, Lantau Island, Hong Kong, p. 3550 3388, 大嶼山香港迪士尼樂園

4. Flow Academy Aqua Park

Escape the hustle and bustle of the city by going to Flow Academy Aqua Park in the beautiful countryside of Tai Po. The Aqua Park is a grand addition to Flow Academy’s water sport facilities, which include paddle-boating and kayaking. With 3,000 square metres of water activity space, the inflatable park consists of 55 interconnected floating components, including 15 obstacle tracks, a ten-metre-tall water slide, monkey bars, swings, hurdles, and more. Sounds exciting!

Flow Academy Aqua Park, Lake Egret Nature Park, 2 Hung Lam Drive, Tai Po Kau, Tai Po, Hong Kong, p. 6762 5303

5. Water World Ocean Park

As part of the long-awaited rejuvenation of Ocean Park, Hongkongers’ have been eagerly awaiting the opening of Water World. This family-friendly water park, located on the waterfront, provides plenty of fun and waves all year round, with nine different slides in five zones. Get topsy-turvy at the Torrential River zone, with exciting rides such as the Tropical Twist, Vortex!, and Cavern Chase. For the thrill-seekers, look no further than the Thrill Valley, where you’ll be able to free-fall from the Bravery Cliffs and Daredevil Drop.

Water World Ocean Park, 33 Ocean Drive, Aberdeen, Hong Kong, p. 3923 2323, 香港仔海洋徑33號海洋公園水上樂園

6. Cube O Discovery Park

Ocean Park is no longer the only place with amazing aquariums and colourful jellyfish! Newly opened in September 2021, Cube O Discovery Park offers a glimpse into the rich biodiversity of the underwater world. Not only are there kid-friendly workshops and educational activities about marine conservation, there are also 15 themed zones, interactive games, a playhouse, and a cafe. But, the highlight has got to be the array of jellyfish displays, including a giant kaleidoscope, mixed reality jellyfish classroom, and more.

Cube O Discovery Park, 8/F, Plaza 88, 88 Yeung Uk Road, Tsuen Wan, Hong Kong, p. 2127 4188, 荃灣楊屋道88號 Plaza 88 8樓浩海立方・探游館
